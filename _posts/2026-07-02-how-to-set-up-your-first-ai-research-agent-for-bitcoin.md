## What an AI Research Agent Actually Does for Bitcoin

Worth understanding before you build one.

A research agent is an autonomous AI you configure once and schedule to run on a recurring basis. It pulls real-time data, analyzes it against your parameters, and delivers personalized insights — without you logging in to manually review dashboards. The agent does that work and surfaces what matters.

For Bitcoin, that means on-chain data. On-chain data is activity recorded directly on the Bitcoin blockchain: how many coins are moving, where they're moving from, whether long-term holders are accumulating or distributing. These signals often move ahead of price. Institutional desks pay to monitor this. With an agent, it gets delivered to your phone automatically.

## Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your First Bitcoin Research Agent

### Step 1: Download the App and Create Your Account (2 minutes)

Download Agenticly from the App Store or Google Play. Open it and tap **Get Started for Free**. The free tier gives you full access to agent setup with no upfront payment.

Onboarding is short. You're not connecting wallets or brokerage accounts at this stage — just creating your account.

### Step 2: Start a New Agent (1 minute)

Inside the app, navigate to the agent creation section and tap to create a new agent. You'll be prompted to name it. Something like "Bitcoin Research" works fine.

This is your agent's identity. Once you're comfortable, you can run multiple agents at once, each focused on different assets or research angles.

### Step 3: Select Bitcoin as Your Focus Asset (1 minute)

Choose Bitcoin as the primary asset. Agenticly decodes on-chain data for Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana — so you're working with a platform that reads actual blockchain activity, not just price feeds.

For your first agent, keep it narrow. Bitcoin only. You can build out Ethereum or Solana agents separately once this one is running.

### Step 4: Configure Your Research Parameters (3 minutes)

This is where you tell the agent what to look for. For a Bitcoin research agent, consider focusing on:

- **On-chain activity signals** — large wallet movements, shifts in holder behavior
- **Price and momentum context** — where Bitcoin sits relative to recent ranges
- **Market structure summaries** — plain-language reads on what's actually happening

You don't need to configure every option on your first run. Pick the categories that match how you think about Bitcoin, whether that's accumulation patterns, short-term price context, or broader market conditions.

The agent tailors its output to what you've selected. You won't get generic market commentary — you'll get what you asked for.

### Step 5: Set Your Schedule (2 minutes)

This is what separates an agent from a static dashboard. You can schedule it to run automatically — every morning before markets open, daily at a set time, whatever fits your routine.

Daily is a solid starting point for Bitcoin research. The agent runs at your chosen time, processes the latest data, and has insights ready when you check your phone.

The market never sleeps. Neither do your agents.

### Step 6: Run Your First Agent and Review the Output (1 minute)

Trigger a manual run to see your first output right away. You'll get a structured summary — not a wall of raw data.

If the output isn't quite what you were hoping for, adjust your parameters. That's normal. Most investors refine their configuration after the first run or two.

## What Good Bitcoin Agent Output Looks Like

A well-configured agent should deliver:

- A clear on-chain summary written in plain language, not raw metrics
- Relevant price context without the noise
- Specific signals worth watching, not a generic "Bitcoin is up/down" recap

What it shouldn't do is flood you with every available data point. The whole point of an agent is to filter. If your output feels too broad, narrow your parameters.
